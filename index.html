Branding

What do we do?

We sell cockroaches! In any quantity. Want a single cockroach? We got you. Want a dozen? We still got you. Maybe even a hundred cockroaches? We got your back!

What can we offer you?

  • A great time
  • Lots of fun!

In all seriousness, have you ever wanted to send a package of something not-so-nice to a not-so-nice person in your life? Roaches are one of the most resilient life-forms on the planet. And what could possibly be worse than a cockroach infestation? If you said white man studying political science, you are correct but we can't send those through mail, so we settled for the next best thing. Revenge? We got your back! (We recommend at least 30 cockroaches in this instance.) A fun prank? We got your back! (We recommend 5 or less cockroaches in this instant.) Wanting to feed your pet reptile? We also got your back!

Who are you?

You are a sensitive, thoughtful person who wishes to inflict pain onto someone, but in a way that doesn't cause result in serious consequences. Keying their car? Property destruction? Setting their belongings on fire? Classics, of course, but those can potentially lead to some not so fun lawsuits, backlash from people around you, and other dire consequences.

Layout Inspiration

Potential Moodboard/Colour Palette/Fonts

I wanted the moodboard and layout to go for a more gritty/retro aesthetic. When I think about bugs, I think about elementary school, spending time playing in dirt, and rolling around in grass. This brings me back to filling out worksheets mass printed from copier machines. that would roughly scan pages from textbooks. The pages would be filled with splotchy ink marks, halftone dots evident on any illustrations, and I think it's just a very charming aesthetic. I wanted to emulate that within my website, so the fonts I aim to use for headlines have a shaky flow of lines, the type to be used in old newspapers which I find very nostalgic. I aim to market towards the quirky kind of teenage girls who want to have fun but also have a little demon inside them telling them to drop porcelain cups whenever they pick one up. They go to therapy regularly, but probably not quite enough.
